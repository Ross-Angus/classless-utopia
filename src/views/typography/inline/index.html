Inline text

Text edits

Three HTML tags are related to editing of text - ins, del and mark. These are intended to show where a document has changed. Taking inspiration of the comments feature of modern word processors, these have been styled to sit outside the main body of the text on desktop.

This is an example ins tag. It always appears to the left of the main article.The first of these - ins - is used to pull a short piece of text out of the main paragraph and attach it to the left hand margin. This is like a footnote but with less scrolling required by the user. This is an example del tag. It always appears to the right of the main article.The del tag works exactly the same, but appears to the right of the text.

These two tags can also have what appears to be a heading at the top. This string is pulled from the title attribute and surfaced for the reader. Examples of both techniques can be seen in the example text below.

inst does not always need a title attribute.The del element doesn't need to have a title attribute either.Because both the del and ins tags are removed from the document flow, care should be taken to not add too many in quick succession or they will collide and make the text illegible.

When the width of the site means there is no longer enough room for them to appear to the side of the text, they float to the right and left instead, leaving a gutter.

The mark element is used to highlight text on the page.
